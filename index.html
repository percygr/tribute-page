Sun Facts:

  • The sun is a large ball of hot plasma, primarily made up of hydrogen and helium
  • It is the closest star to Earth and is the center of our solar system
  • The sun is a main-sequence star and is currently in the middle of its life cycle
  • The sun has a diameter of approximately 1.39 million kilometers, making it 109 times larger than Earth
  • It has a mass that is 330,000 times greater than Earth's and accounts for 99.86% of the total mass of the solar system
  • The sun produces light and heat through nuclear fusion reactions in its core, releasing vast amounts of energy
  • The sun's surface temperature is approximately 5,500 degrees Celsius
  • The sun rotates on its axis once every 25-35 days, depending on its latitude
  • The sun has a powerful magnetic field that gives rise to sunspots and solar flares
  • The sun is critical for life on Earth, providing the energy that drives our weather and climate, and supporting photosynthesis in plants
